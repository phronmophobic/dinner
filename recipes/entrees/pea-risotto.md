# Spring Pea & Mushroom Risotto

A bright, creamy spring risotto with sweet peas, savory mushrooms, lemon, and herbs.

- **Serves:** 4
- **Time:** ~45 minutes
- **Diet:** Vegetarian, gluten-free (check stock)

## Ingredients

### Base
- 1.25 L (5 cups) vegetable stock, kept hot (more as needed)
- 3 Tbsp olive oil, divided
- 1 small yellow onion, finely diced (about 1 cup)
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 1/2 cups (300 g) Arborio or Carnaroli rice
- 1/2 cup (120 ml) dry white wine (optional; replace with extra stock)
- 1 tsp kosher salt, plus more to taste
- Fresh black pepper

### Mushrooms
- 340 g (12 oz) mushrooms (cremini, button, or a mix), sliced
- 1 Tbsp butter (or additional olive oil)
- 1 tsp soy sauce or tamari (optional, boosts umami)

### Spring finish
- 1 1/2 cups (about 225 g) peas (fresh shelled or frozen)
- 2 Tbsp butter (or olive oil)
- 1/2 cup (50 g) finely grated Parmesan (plus more to serve)
- 1 tsp lemon zest
- 2–3 Tbsp lemon juice (to taste)
- 2 Tbsp chopped fresh herbs (mint + parsley is great; or dill)
- Optional: 2 Tbsp toasted pine nuts or sliced almonds

## Instructions

1. **Heat the stock.**  
   Warm the vegetable stock in a pot and keep it at a gentle simmer. (Hot stock helps the risotto cook evenly.)

2. **Brown the mushrooms.**  
   In a wide skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat, add **1 Tbsp olive oil**.  
   Add mushrooms and cook until they release their liquid and get browned, 8–10 minutes.  
   Add **1 Tbsp butter** (or oil), a pinch of salt, and **soy sauce/tamari** if using; cook 30 seconds more.  
   Transfer mushrooms to a bowl; set aside.

3. **Sauté aromatics.**  
   Reduce heat to medium. Add remaining **2 Tbsp olive oil** to the same pan.  
   Cook onion with a pinch of salt until translucent, 4–6 minutes.  
   Add garlic and cook 30 seconds.

4. **Toast the rice.**  
   Add rice and stir constantly until the grains look slightly translucent at the edges, 1–2 minutes.

5. **Deglaze.**  
   Add wine (if using) and stir until mostly absorbed.

6. **Ladle and stir.**  
   Add hot stock about **1/2 cup at a time**, stirring frequently.  
   When the liquid is mostly absorbed, add another ladle.  
   Continue 18–25 minutes, until rice is **tender but still a little firm** (al dente).  
   (You may not use all the stock, or you may need a splash more.)

7. **Add peas and mushrooms.**  
   Stir in peas and the reserved mushrooms. Cook 2–3 minutes until peas are hot and bright.

8. **Finish (“mantecatura”).**  
   Turn off heat. Stir in **2 Tbsp butter**, **Parmesan**, **lemon zest**, and **2 Tbsp lemon juice**.  
   Loosen with a splash of stock if needed—the ideal risotto is creamy and “flowing,” not stiff.  
   Season with salt and pepper to taste.

9. **Herbs + serve.**  
   Fold in herbs right before serving. Top with extra Parmesan and nuts (optional).  
   Serve immediately.

## Notes

- **No-wine version:** Skip wine and just use stock; add an extra squeeze of lemon at the end to brighten.
- **Frozen peas:** Work perfectly—no need to thaw first.
- **Make it vegan:** Use olive oil instead of butter; use a vegan Parmesan or 2–3 Tbsp nutritional yeast + extra salt to taste.
- **Best mushrooms:** Cremini + shiitake is excellent if you want deeper flavor.
